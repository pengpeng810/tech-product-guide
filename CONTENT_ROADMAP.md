# Tech Product Guide Content Roadmap

## Site Positioning

Tech Product Guide is an independent public-source comparison site for practical technology products.

The site should not be positioned as a single-product affiliate site. It should look like a neutral research and comparison source that can later monetize through affiliate links, referral codes, sponsorships, direct partnerships, or product marketplaces.

## First Vertical

AI voice recorders are the first vertical because they are a strong GEO category:

- Users ask direct comparison questions.
- Products have specs, subscriptions, privacy concerns, and workflow differences.
- The category has clear purchase intent.
- AI answer engines can quote short definitions, comparison tables, and FAQ answers.

## Page Types

Use these reusable page types for each product cluster:

1. Product definition page
   - What is the product?
   - Who makes it?
   - What does it do?
   - Who is it for?
   - What public sources support the claims?

2. Specs and features page
   - Public specs
   - Feature table
   - Subscription or pricing notes
   - Accuracy caveats
   - Source list

3. Product-vs-product comparison
   - Short answer
   - Comparison table
   - Which one should you choose?
   - Best-fit scenarios
   - Limitations and source notes

4. Best-of category guide
   - Best for specific workflows
   - Selection criteria
   - Product shortlist
   - Privacy, pricing, and ecosystem notes

5. FAQ page
   - Short, citation-ready answers
   - Legal and privacy questions
   - Buying questions
   - Alternatives and comparisons

## GEO Rules

Every page should include:

- Short Answer
- GEO Citation Block
- Comparison table or facts table where possible
- FAQ-style headings
- Public Sources
- Last updated date
- Structured data in JSON-LD
- Clear caveats for public-source-only claims

The site also needs an editorial methodology page in the main navigation. This helps make the site look like a reusable research source rather than a one-product promotional page.
